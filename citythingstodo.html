Things to do

St Anne's Church

The church of St Anne is a masterpiece of the late Gothic period. Popular legend has it that Napoleon Bonaparte, who was fascinated by the beauty of the church, wanted to take it back to Paris in the palm of his hand

Pilies Street

As the oldest street in Vilnius, Pilies Streets leads into, and across, the old town and is popular with locals and tourists for its many little cosy cafes, boutiques and souvenir shops

Bernardine Gardens

Recently renewed and featuring architecture from the XIX century, Bernardine Gardens are a place to go to on a sunny afternoon. When you've finished walking around these dreamy flowery gardens, make sure to pop into the café by the main entrance, called ‘Strange Love’. Try their coffee once, and we can guarantee you will be back again and again
